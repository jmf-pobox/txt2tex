\section*{Syntax Environment Examples}

\section*{Example 1 : Simple Enumeration}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Example 1 : Simple Enumeration}

\noindent For simple free types with several branches, syntax provides

\bigskip

\noindent column-aligned layout:

\bigskip

\begin{syntax}
  OP & ::= & plus | minus | times | divide
\end{syntax}

\noindent This generates aligned columns with the ::= separator clearly visible.

\bigskip

\section*{Example 2 : Parameterized Constructors}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Example 2 : Parameterized Constructors}

\noindent Constructors can have type parameters using angle brackets:

\bigskip

\begin{syntax}
  Tree & ::= & leaf \ldata \nat \rdata | branch \ldata Tree \cross Tree \rdata
\end{syntax}

\noindent Parameters are rendered with \ldata and \rdata $delimiters \in LaTeX$.

\bigskip

\section*{Example 3 : Multiple Definitions with Grouping}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Example 3 : Multiple Definitions with Grouping}

\noindent Blank lines between definitions create visual groups using
\textbackslash also:

\bigskip

\begin{syntax}
  Status & ::= & active | inactive | pending \\
  Response & ::= & ok | error \ldata \nat \rdata | timeout
\end{syntax}

\noindent The blank line causes \also to be generated, creating vertical

\bigskip

\noindent separation between the two type definitions.

\bigskip

\section*{Example 4 : Complex BNF - Style Definitions}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Example 4 : Complex BNF - Style Definitions}

\noindent Syntax environment is ideal for BNF-style grammar definitions:

\bigskip

\begin{syntax}
  Expr & ::= & const \ldata \nat \rdata | var \ldata NAME \rdata |
  binop \ldata OP \cross Expr \cross Expr \rdata \\
  Stmt & ::= & assign \ldata NAME \cross Expr \rdata | seq \ldata
  Stmt \cross Stmt \rdata | skip
\end{syntax}

\noindent This creates a professionally formatted grammar specification.

\bigskip

\section*{Comparison : Syntax vs Zed Blocks}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Comparison : Syntax vs Zed Blocks}

\noindent Standard zed blocks work for simple types:

\bigskip

\begin{zed}
  Color ::= red | green | blue
\end{zed}

\noindent But syntax environment provides better alignment for complex types:

\bigskip

\begin{syntax}
  Color & ::= & red | green | blue \\
  Shape & ::= & circle \ldata \nat \rdata | rectangle \ldata \nat
  \cross \nat \rdata | polygon \ldata \seq~\nat \rdata
\end{syntax}

\noindent The column alignment makes the structure more readable.

\bigskip
