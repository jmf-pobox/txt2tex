=== PAGEBREAK Examples ===

** Example 1: Basic Page Break **

TEXT: This content appears on the first page.

PAGEBREAK

TEXT: This content appears on the next page. The PAGEBREAK directive forces a page break at that point in the document.

** Example 2: Separating Solutions **

TEXT: When preparing solutions for submission, you may want each solution on a separate page.

** Solution 1 **

PAGEBREAK

** Solution 2 **

TEXT: Second solution content here.

exists y : N | y > 10

** Solution 3 **

TEXT: Third solution content here.

** Example 4: Long Proofs **

TEXT: For lengthy proofs, you might want to start each major proof on a new page:

** Theorem 1 **

PROOF:
(p land (p => q)) => q [=> intro from 1]
  [1] p land (p => q) [assumption]
      :: p [land elim 1]
      :: p => q [land elim 2]
      q [=> elim]

TEXT: End of proof for Theorem 1.

PAGEBREAK

** Theorem 2 **

PROOF:
(p lor q) land (lnot q) => p [=> intro from 1]
  [1] (p lor q) land (lnot q) [assumption]
      p lor q [land elim 1]
      lnot q [land elim 2]
      p [lor elim]

TEXT: End of proof for Theorem 2.

** Example 6: Avoiding Orphans **

TEXT: When you have a section title or definition that would appear at the bottom of a page with its content on the next page, you might insert a PAGEBREAK before the section.

PAGEBREAK

=== Important Definition ===

axdef
  critical : N -> N
where
  forall n : N | critical(n) = n * n
end

TEXT: This ensures the definition stays with its title.
